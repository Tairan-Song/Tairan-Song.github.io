I am a Ph.D. student in the MIDI Lab at Michigan State University, advised by Dr. Adam Alessio. My research lies at the intersection of medical image analysis, statistical modeling, and deep learning, with a primary focus on CT-based kidney cancer imaging.

I develop machine learning methods that are robust to real-world clinical heterogeneity, including variability in contrast timing, acquisition protocols, and anatomical context. My work emphasizes radiomics-based modeling, multi-organ contextual feature representations, and clinically deployable pipelines for oncologic imaging and prognosis.

Selected Research

  • Kidney Cancer Grade Prediction from Heterogeneous Contrast-Enhanced CT
    Proposed a radiomics-based modeling framework for renal tumor grading that is robust to highly variable contrast timing in real-world abdominal CT. By incorporating quantitative descriptors from surrounding anatomical regions (e.g., normal kidney and major vessels), the approach improves grading stability without relying on explicit phase labeling or normalization.
  • De Novo Identification of Expressed Somatic Mutations from Single-Cell RNA Sequencing
    Contributed to the development of the RESA computational framework for high-precision identification of expressed somatic mutations from scRNA-seq data. The work integrates statistical modeling and empirical validation to improve mutation detection sensitivity and precision across diverse datasets.
